Data-Driven Control & Trajectory Optimization

19 May 2024 Atlanta, USA
Call for papers

About the Workshop

Data-driven techniques have demonstrated significant empirical success in addressing many challenging robotic problems continuously scaling up the complexity and pushing the boundaries of robotic autonomy in the physical world. Conversely, optimal control and trajectory optimization are foundational tools that tackles smaller scale problems, they prioritize aspects such as stability, robustness, safety, and precision. A fundamental research question in robotics, particularly in the era of foundation models, is:

How can we effectively leverage the strengths of both data-driven methods and traditional control approaches to achieve agile and intelligent robotic autonomy while ensuring safety and robustness?

This workshop aims to provide an overview of recent advancements in integrating data-driven techniques with model-based optimization methods to achieve state-of-the-art robotic autonomy. The invited talks and workshop papers will cover a broad range of foundational knowledge in robotics, such as reinforcement learning and optimal control, while emphasizing their application to tackling real-world challenges, including agile navigation. The workshop will be highly beneficial for researchers seeking to explore cutting-edge methodologies, engineers looking for practical solutions to complex robotic challenges, and students aiming to build a solid foundation in both data-driven and traditional control approaches. By bridging theory and practice, the workshop will offer valuable insights and networking opportunities for participants at all stages of their academic and professional careers.
