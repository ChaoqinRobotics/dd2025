About The Workshop

As autonomous systems become increasingly integral across different domains, the demand for advanced control and trajectory optimization techniques for real-world deployment has become more essential. Recent advancements in data-driven techniques provide a powerful tool to push the boundaries of precision and adaptability beyond the limits of traditional systems. In addition, its application for refining control algorithms, developing predictive models, and optimizing trajectories has demonstrated its promise in addressing real-world challenges in control and trajectory optimization.


This workshop will focus on integrating learning-based approaches, foundation models, and theory of optimization and control with real-world data to improve the performance and adaptability of autonomous systems. We will provide a platform for researchers, engineers, and practitioners to share their insights on the use of cutting-edge data-driven techniques for traditional and established research problems in control and optimization. Participants will leave with a deeper understanding of how these innovations can be applied to improve real-world deployments in autonomy, from robotics and drones to vehicles and beyond.



Objectives


The goal of this workshop is to provide an opportunity for cross-disciplinary research discussion and exchange of ideas to bridge traditionally distinct areas in control and trajectory optimization. Such an objective is aligned with ICRA’s goal of discussing emerging robotics techniques.



Originality and Diversity


There are several control and trajectory optimization workshops, but none of them focuses on drone autonomy with data-driven approaches.



Estimated Attendance:

The proposed workshop covers the areas in a lot of traditional distinct committees that are working on similar problems and encourages discussion and collaboration across these groups to develop more integrated solutions. The topics are related to many IEEE RAS technical committees:

  • Machine Learning for Automation: data-driven methods are one of the mainstream techniques to bridge the lab-to-real gaps for robots’ real-world autonomy.
  • Algorithms for Planning and Control of Robot Motion: data-driven methods have demonstrated advantages for the deployment of planning and control algorithms.
  • Aerial Robotics and Unmanned Aerial Vehicles: since one of the main applications of control and trajectory optimization is for drone navigation.
  • Robot Learning: Robots can use data-driven methods to learn from, various sources, including demonstrations, interactions, and other types of data.
  • Model-Based Optimization for Robotics: hybrid approaches are often employed with data-driven techniques within a model-based framework.

There are also some related workshops in learning for planning, data-driven control, and trajectory optimization, but none of them address the integration of these topics.

  • ICRA 2021: Machine Learning for Motion Planning Workshop (3 spotlight papers, 8 regular papers)
  • Humanoids 2022: Advancements in Trajectory Optimization and Model Predictive Control for Legged Systems (4 poster abstracts)
  • CDC 2024: Data-driven control: theory and applications (upcoming).
  • ICRA 2024: Advancements in Trajectory Optimization and Model Predictive Control for Legged Systems 2nd Edition

Referring to the attendance of previous related workshops, we expect around 100-200 attendance, 10-20 abstract (or paper) submissions, and 8-16 workshop submissions.
