About The Workshop

Abstract

Data-driven techniques have demonstrated significant empirical success in addressing many challenging robotic problems continuously scaling up the complexity and pushing the boundaries of robotic autonomy in the physical world. Conversely, optimal control and trajectory optimization are foundational tools that tackles smaller scale problems, they prioritize aspects such as stability, robustness, safety, and precision. A fundamental research question in robotics, particularly in the era of foundation models, is:


How can we effectively leverage the strengths of both data-driven methods and traditional control approaches to achieve agile and intelligent robotic autonomy while ensuring safety and robustness?


This workshop aims to provide an overview of recent advancements in integrating data-driven techniques with model-based optimization methods to achieve state-of-the-art robotic autonomy. The invited talks and workshop papers will cover a broad range of foundational knowledge in robotics, such as reinforcement learning and optimal control, while emphasizing their application to tackling real-world challenges, including agile navigation. The workshop will be highly beneficial for researchers seeking to explore cutting-edge methodologies, engineers looking for practical solutions to complex robotic challenges, and students aiming to build a solid foundation in both data-driven and traditional control approaches. By bridging theory and practice, the workshop will offer valuable insights and networking opportunities for participants at all stages of their academic and professional careers.



The Content of the Workshop


Relevance and Objectives


This workshop aims to provide an opportunity for cross-disciplinary researchers to share insights on data-driven control and optimization techniques crucial for developing next-generation autonomous robotic systems. As robots have been receiving high expectations to operate in complex and dynamic environments, the research towards autonomy has expanded beyond the robot’s stability, robustness, dissipativity, and optimality. Keywords such as agility, self-adaptability, real-time efficiency, and general-purpose functionality have captured public attention. With the growing demand for robots to perform in complex and ever-changing environments, the scope of autonomous research has broadened beyond ensuring stability, robustness, and optimal performance. There is now a strong emphasis on developing robots that can respond swiftly, adapt to new situations, operate efficiently in real-time, handle a wide range of tasks seamlessly, and ultimately achieve a general-purpose system. Such a paradigm shift has a bright future thanks to the combination of novel learning-based methods with classical control and optimization theories, including but not limited to, reinforcement learning in simulation, regression methods for modeling, neural network approximations for online solving, and Large Language Models (LLMs) in the loop. Such a paradigm shift also demands researchers and students to develop a fundamental understanding of diverse topics across different disciplines, including but not limited to reinforcement learning, machine learning, numerical optimization for control and planning, and Large Foundation Models.


This workshop aims to bring researchers who think rigorously across those emerging disciplines and to foster collaborative discussions on how to effectively use data to drive autonomy to the next level.


By bridging experts from diverse fields, this workshop aims to foster collaborative discussion and innovative research on how to use data to drive autonomy to the next level. The goal of this workshop is aligned with ICRA’s goal of discussing emerging robotics techniques.



Originality and Diversity


Existing robotics workshops in control and trajectory optimization seldom focus on autonomy with data-driven approaches. This workshop will fill in the gap by concentrating on the intersection of data-driven methods with control and optimization for robotics. Our topic selection naturally attracts a diverse range of academia and industry attention not only from the field of robotics but also from control systems, machine learning, reinforcement learning, and optimization.



Estimated Attendance:

The proposed workshop covers the areas in a lot of traditional distinct committees that are working on similar problems and encourages discussion and collaboration across these groups to develop more integrated solutions. The topics are related to many IEEE RAS technical committees:

  • Machine Learning for Automation: data-driven methods are one of the mainstream techniques to bridge the lab-to-real gaps for robots’ real-world autonomy.
  • Algorithms for Planning and Control of Robot Motion: data-driven methods have demonstrated advantages for the deployment of planning and control algorithms.
  • Aerial Robotics and Unmanned Aerial Vehicles: aerial robots are among the most flexible and accessible platforms for collecting data and validating performance improvements through data-driven methods.
  • Model-Based Optimization for Robotics: hybrid approaches are often employed with data-driven techniques within a model-based framework.
  • Multi-Robot Systems: sharing data within multiple robots can effectively boost the intelligence of the swarm system.
  • Micro/Nano Robotics and Automation: limited by the actuation power, small robots rely on the proper use of data to fight against disturbance.
  • Verification of Autonomous Systems: data is key to advancing the analysis and test processes to provide increased confidence in autonomous systems.
  • Robot Control: introduce data-driven methods to robotic modeling and control.

There are also some related workshops in learning for planning, data-driven control, and trajectory optimization, but none of them address the integration of these topics.

  • ICRA 2021: Machine Learning for Motion Planning Workshop (3 spotlight papers, 8 regular papers)
  • Humanoids 2022: Advancements in Trajectory Optimization and Model Predictive Control for Legged Systems (4 poster abstracts)
  • CDC 2024: Data-driven control: theory and applications (upcoming).
  • ICRA 2024: Advancements in Trajectory Optimization and Model Predictive Control for Legged Systems 2nd Edition

Referring to the attendance of previous related workshops, we expect around 100-200 attendance, 10-20 abstract (or paper) submissions, and 8-16 workshop submissions.


In addition, theses related workshops only focus on one side of data-driven or learning-based methods or traditional trajectory optimization, but none of them address the integration of these topics in robotics.
